# Pixel Illustration

Style and production rules for making Brand pixel/isometric illustration
images.

## Style Definition

Use for topics that need warmth, context, or a less stiff entry point.

## Best For

- seller confusion
- settlement and fees
- policy explainers
- beginner-seller behavior
- abstract data topics

## Core Production Pattern

- Use a dark isometric workspace or compact seller-operation scene.
- Keep the illustration friendly but still useful and business-oriented.
- Use a small store/seller mascot, receipts, calculator, laptop, chart blocks,
  shelves, or operational objects.
- Use Brand-blue as the active accent. Keep any other color restrained.
- Keep the image clean: no text, no logo, no final card layout, no baked
  gradient.
- Leave the lower 35% calmer and darker for the hook headline.
- Use warm light with Brand-blue accents.

## Prompt Blocks To Reuse

- `dark isometric workspace`
- `soft editorial pixel-art inspired illustration`
- `friendly store mascot`
- `seller operation objects`
- `Brand-blue accents`
- `lower 35% visually darker and simpler for Korean headline overlay`

## Common Risks

- Becoming too cute or game-like.
- Looking like generic pixel art instead of seller operations.
- Overusing green or decorative colors.
- Adding readable fake labels to receipts, screens, or signs.
