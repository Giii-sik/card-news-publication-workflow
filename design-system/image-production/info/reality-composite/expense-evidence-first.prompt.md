# Expense Evidence First Reality Composite

## Purpose

Info-card top image for a card explaining that expense processing starts with
evidence: receipts, invoices, and transaction records.

Sample proof:

- `design-system/samples/info-image-system/output/01_info_expense_evidence_reality_composite.png`
- Source asset: `design-system/samples/info-image-system/assets/01-info-reality-composite-clean.png`

## Visual Direction

Photorealistic editorial composite with a Korean ecommerce seller at a desk.
The person is the emotional anchor. An exaggerated evidence folder or archive
box full of blank receipts makes the business point visible before the lower
text is read.

This follows the uploaded reference pattern:

- person reaction in the foreground
- one surreal but readable metaphor object
- dark editorial lighting
- clean lower card text handled by HTML, not the image

## Reusable Prompt

Create a clean source image for an Instagram card-news info image area. Aspect
ratio 1080x819 or taller vertical source that crops well into 1080x819.

Photorealistic editorial composite in a Korean ecommerce office at night. A
small online seller sits at a desk with a laptop, parcel boxes, calculator,
file folders, receipts, invoices, and expense documents. The seller looks
focused but slightly overwhelmed while holding a blank receipt and sorting
paper evidence. Behind the seller, two office assistants carry an exaggerated
oversized transparent evidence folder or archive box overflowing with blank
receipts, invoices, and parcel slips, suggesting that proof documents must come
before expense processing. Cinematic moody lighting, believable shadows,
premium news-thumbnail energy, foreground sharp and background slightly softer.
The scene should feel realistic even though the oversized evidence folder is
exaggerated.

No readable Korean or English text. No real brand logos. No exact numbers. No
watermark. No final card layout. No typography baked into the image.

## Notes

- The image explains evidence pressure, not tax-law details.
- Exact facts, terms, and dates must stay in the card text or caption.
- If a new generated version is made, keep the same role split: image carries
  the situation; HTML carries the business message.
