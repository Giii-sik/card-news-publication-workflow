## What To Borrow

- Use a person as the emotional anchor.
- Put one exaggerated metaphor object behind or beside the person.
- Keep the scene photographic and believable even when the metaphor is absurd.
- Avoid literal UI mockups. The image should express pressure, confusion,
  urgency, or opportunity through people and props.
- Leave final Korean copy to HTML. Do not generate Korean text, exact numbers,
  or brand logos inside the image.

## What To Avoid

- Do not reuse the uploaded images as final assets.
- Do not copy recognizable brand logos, celebrities, or protected characters.
- Do not make the image so dramatic that it feels like crime/news panic.
- Do not let the image explain every detail. It should set the situation; the
  lower text box explains the business meaning.

## 3D Graphic Direction

- For quiet explainers, small object characters on a real desk are still useful.
- For platform rivalry or news scenes, small clean desk objects are too weak.
  Use cinematic mascot action: large characters, strong expressions, clear
  chase/defense/repair action, dense topic-specific background, and motion cues.
- Brand cues must be characterized. Avoid direct brand logos by default; when
  approved, integrate the cue into the body shape, color blocking, molded mark,
  or printed surface. Do not paste flat white labels onto a finished character.
- A good 3D action reference should feel like a still from a scene, not a
  product shot of a toy.
