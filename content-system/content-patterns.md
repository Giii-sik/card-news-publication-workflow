# Content Patterns

## Recommended Carousel Shapes

### 5-Card Information

Use this as the default v1 shape for beginner seller content. It shares useful
information first and closes with the fixed Brand follow CTA.

1. Hook: image plus short sentence
2. Problem: a concrete beginner seller situation
3. Interpretation: the one thing to understand
4. Application: a copyable checklist, sentence, or operating rule
5. CTA: fixed follow card for `@your_brand`

### 3-Card List

Use for quick checklists or simple how-to posts.

1. Hook
2. Info list
3. CTA

### 5-Card Narrative

Use for market shifts, fee changes, strategy reframing, and seller decision topics.

1. Hook
2. Problem
3. Perspective
4. Solution or checklist
5. CTA

## Card Rules

- Audience default: beginner sellers.
- Ask: "Would a beginner seller lose something useful by scrolling past this?"
- One idea per card.
- Problem cards must show a concrete seller situation, not a general market statement.
- Info cards should state the takeaway directly in the `h3` headline.
- Every card should create a small reason to continue to the next card.
- Keep highlights as exact substrings.
- Keep evidence quiet and short.
- Final CTA is fixed as the Brand follow card. Do not create alternate CTA
  styles unless the user explicitly asks for a new template.
- Mention Brand only after the useful content has landed.

## Source Rules

- Do not invent sources.
- Browse when facts, product names, dates, fees, or market claims may have changed.
- Prefer official docs, primary announcements, and clearly dated sources.
- Internal observation notes can be used for Track E, but label them as internal-note sources in the spec.
