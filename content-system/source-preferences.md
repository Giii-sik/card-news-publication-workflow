# Source Preferences

Rules for researching and citing sources.

## Source Tiers

1. Official/primary source
   - platform notice
   - product documentation
   - company announcement
   - government or regulatory source
2. Primary business material
   - earnings release
   - investor presentation
   - official blog or newsroom
3. Dated reporting
   - reputable news article
   - dated industry report
4. Community or field observation
   - seller community posts
   - internal Brand observation
   - user-provided anecdote

## Usage Rules

- For policies, dates, fees, prices, rankings, tool capabilities, and product
  changes, use Tier 1 whenever possible.
- Tier 3 can explain context but should not be the only source for a precise
  policy or numeric claim.
- Tier 4 is useful for pain discovery, but label it as observation, not fact.
- Do not invent sources.
- Always preserve source title, URL, and publication or access date when known.

## Source Ledger Format

Use this when handing research to writers:

```text
Fact:
Source:
URL:
Date:
Used in card:
Confidence: high | medium | low
Notes:
```
